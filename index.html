The Second WORLD WAR

The Treaty of Versailles

The Treaty of Versailles was the result of the first World War, signed during 1919-1920, during the German-Allied Armistice, resulting in a large loss of territory for the German Empire, losing out on the territories of Elsass-Loringthen, Danzig, Schleswig, Poznan, The Saar, Katowice, and Memel. The newly formed Weimar Republic was also limited to 100,000 troops, not allowed to build an airforce, and the Rhineland was demilitarized.
